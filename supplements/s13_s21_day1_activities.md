## Activity 1: Add Context to a Vague Prompt
*Slide reference: s13 — Context Changes Everything*
Time: 10 minutes

### Background
A vague prompt gives AI a vague answer. Adding context (who you are, what you need, and how you want the answer) makes the output dramatically more useful.

### Your Task
Take each vague prompt below. Rewrite it as a better, context-rich prompt.

| # | Vague Prompt | Your Improved Version |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Explain databases" | |
| 2 | "Help me write an email" | |
| 3 | "What is Python?" | |
| 4 | "Summarize this topic" | |

### Guide: What to Add
- **Who you are:** "I am an IT student…"
- **What you need:** "…who needs to understand X for an exam"
- **Format you want:** "Explain it in simple bullet points, no jargon"
- **Length:** "In 3 sentences" or "In under 100 words"

### Example
- **Vague:** "Tell me about networking"
- **Better:** "I am a first-year IT student. Explain computer networking in simple terms. Cover what it is, why it matters, and give one real-life example. Use bullet points and keep it under 150 words."

### Discussion
After rewriting your prompts, share one with the class. Compare: how different is the AI's response to the vague vs. improved version?

## Activity 2: Write a Prompt from Scratch
*Slide reference: s18 — Prompt Writing Practice*
Time: 15 minutes

### Your Task
Choose a real task from the list below (or make up your own). Write a complete prompt for it using the structure you learned.

**Real tasks to choose from:**
- Ask AI to explain a concept you're studying
- Ask AI to write a short professional email
- Ask AI to create a simple quiz on a topic you choose
- Ask AI to explain a programming error message
- Ask AI to summarize a paragraph from your textbook

### Prompt Structure to Follow

```
Context:   [Who you are and what situation you're in]
Task:      [What you want AI to do — be specific]
Format:    [How you want the answer: bullet points, paragraph, table, etc.]
Limit:     [Any constraint: word count, reading level, no jargon]
```

## Activity 3: Prompt Checklist Self-Assessment
*Slide reference: s21 — Prompt Engineering Checklist*
Time: 5 minutes (individual) + 5 minutes (group)

### Instructions
Look at a prompt you wrote today (from Activity 1 or 2). Check each box that applies.

**Checklist:**

- [ ] My prompt has a clear task — AI knows exactly what to do
- [ ] I included context — AI knows who I am or what situation I'm in
- [ ] I specified a format — I asked for bullet points, a table, steps, etc.
- [ ] I set a constraint — word count, reading level, or tone
- [ ] I used specific language — no vague words like "good", "nice", "stuff"
- [ ] The prompt is one focused request — not 5 things at once

**My score:** _____ / 6

### Improve It
If your score is 4 or less, rewrite your prompt to check all 6 boxes. Then run it again and compare the results.

### Group Reflection
Share your best prompt with a partner. Let them rate it using the checklist (you rate theirs). Discuss: what made the good ones better?
